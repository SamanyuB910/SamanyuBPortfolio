This is a draft. The experiments are finished; the prose is not.

Treewidth measures how far a graph is from being a tree, which makes it a reasonable proxy for how cleanly a computation factors. SAE feature circuits are graphs, and as far as I can tell nobody had measured their treewidth before, so I did. From GPT-2 I extracted 183 co-activation graphs over 150 prompts and asked whether graph structure alone separates honest responses from confabulated ones. It does: 18 structural measures classify the two conditions at 80% accuracy (AUC 0.851), and treewidth alone captures 78.7%. The direction is the interesting part. Honest computation is measurably more entangled than confabulation (Cohen's d = −1.24, p < 1e-8); I would have guessed the reverse. Exact treewidth is expensive to compute, so the scoring path is Numba-accelerated. The pipeline is open-sourced with reproducible configs, and the finished post will walk through it end to end.
